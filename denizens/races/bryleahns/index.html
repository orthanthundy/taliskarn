Bryleahns

Bryleahns are a people born from the blood of Fluere, Mother of All Currents, who settled along the banks of the sacred river Sanguama.

Table of Content

TL;DR Bryleahns – cold-blooded jungle dwellers:
  • Appearance: fin-like ears, yellow-green skin, height from 4 to 6.5 feet (older = taller), no hair, cranial crest, large eyes, broad shoulders, barrel chests, females lack breasts;
  • Lifespan: average 60-70 years;
  • Typical occupations: hunters, alchemists, scholars, philosophers;
  • Philosophy: "Follow the current without altering it";
  • Racial themes: harmony with nature, warmth & water, poisons;
  • Weapons: tridents, blowguns;
  • Cities: traditionalists "weave" homes on giant trees, reformers build human-like structures on elevated ground;
  • Similar races from other universes: Merfolk from MtG, Tritons from... wherever you like :)
D&D Classes: Bryleahns work best as rangers, druids, alchemists, wizards (note: Taliscar has limited magic, but go wild if you want!).

Appearance and Physiology

Bryleahns are lean and wiry humanoids, perfectly adapted to life in the dense and humid jungles of Bryleah. In youth, they are short in stature: by adulthood (reached at their tenth spring), they barely reach 4-4.5 feet. However, their growth never ceases, and by old age, a Bryleahn may grow up to 6-6.5 feet.

Their faces resemble humans’, but large eyes are shielded by a third transparent eyelid, cheeks are sunken, and small mouths are filled with tiny sharp teeth. Heads are large, hairless, with fin-like ears and a short crest stretching from the midpoint of a high forehead to the base of the skull.

Bryleahn blood runs cold and azure, like Sanguama’s waters – hence their ears and crests are densely vascularized to aid temperature regulation. Their skin is thick, dotted with small bumps, and pale yellow in hue.

Bryleahns have broad shoulders and strong arms, long fingers with webbing between the first phalanges. Their spines protrude slightly, forming a dorsal ridge, while agile, elongated legs resemble a cross between monkey and frog limbs.

Bryleahns eat meat and fish, using plants only as seasonings, and cannot tolerate milk. Their young eat meat from birth, so females lack breasts and nipples, though all Fluere’s children have expanded ribcages with pronounced ribs. This allows them to hold their breath for 15-20 minutes.

Culture

Bryleahn temperaments mirror the currents shaped by Fluere: the young resemble rushing streams winding through foliage, while elders flow like a broad, unstoppable river.

Most Bryleahns follow the Cult of Currents:

“Life is like the flow of the great Sanguama: it has its beginning and end. By weaving our fate’s currents with those of other beings, we journey from source to mouth, where we merge with the Mother’s waters.” They naturally seek harmony with the world rather than reshaping it, preserving primal nature. They are a people of philosophers and scholars who study life’s course through observation and contemplation.

Note: Not all interpret the Cult’s tenets alike. For example, Bryleahns in the northern Shallows of the Scar reject clothing and tools, while those in the southern Azure Haven freely chop wood and fire clay. Still, all Bryleahns share a drive to maintain natural harmony.

Material Culture

All Bryleahns are born hunters. Silent as shadows, they prowl Sanguama’s lush banks or dive into its azure waters to ambush prey.

They wield reed blowguns firing poison-tipped thorns with immense force, and harpoons: tridents with long hafts tipped with resin teeth from the lakrikel tree. This resin, specially treated and hardened, shapes other tools: knives, needles, fishhooks, and utensils. Hunters also use sticky vine nets.

Bryleahns wear little clothing, covering only their loins with cloth-like wraps, but adorn themselves with lakrikel resin amulets, bark-and-vine bracelets, and wide woven belts holding pouches and vials. Southerners of Azure Haven add golden bangles and jewelry.

Hunting Mask

Every adult Bryleahn’s greatest treasure is their hunting mask. Bryleahns know the Forest Horror, Moss-Hide, is too cautious to attack face-to-face, so they craft eyes for the back of their heads. Masks are made of animal hides stretched over lakrikel-reinforced frames, ritually painted and adorned with two glowing lapis lazuli eyes – azure stones carried downstream by Sanguama. No Bryleahn leaves their settlement without this mask.

Bryleahns are master alchemists, excelling in herbal medicine and advanced poisoncraft.

They traditionally build settlements on tree trunks, weaving vines and branches without harming the host. Meza – the Woven City, rising on an island in Sanguama’s heart, is one of Malkona’s wonders, home to philosophers, scholars, and poets. Southern Bryleahns, lacking large trees, settle on high river hills, building human-style homes from wood, straw, and clay. –>
