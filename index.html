Taliskarn

The World of Unwritten Maps


Taliskarn is under active development. My native language is Russian, so the site was initially published in Russian, but I’m preparing the English version now.

Imagine a cold and grim northern highland. Giant moss-clad pines tower like a fortress wall, their somber crowns clawing at the leaden sky. Eternal mist swirls above the tranquil surface of white waters—colder than ice—of a lake lost deep within these woods. In the fog among the trees, the ghost of a predator’s shadow flickers now and then, and the oppressive silence is broken only by the distant howl of a wolf or the low, rumbling growl of unseen beasts.

Hundreds of miles to the south stretch endless rust-red steppes. From horizon to horizon lies a rippling sea of copper grass, punctuated only by rare tabletop mountains. Across this parched land, herds of stone bison roam in their eternal migration, while raptors circle lazily overhead—there is no need to hurry here… until the rising thunder of talard hooves echoes and the piercing whistle of bone-adorned steeds signals the swift approach of nomads.

The glittering jungles of Bryleah: a warm realm of currents—here, you never know from which tree a predatory moss_hide might leap or from where a poisoned dart might fly. The scorching sun of the Scarred Lands of Bruna. The toxic fogs of the Fetid Fens. The mind-numbing gloom of Noctaria. The fiery wastes of Flammarg’s Shards.

All of this is Taliskarn: a harsh, unwelcoming, inhospitable, and often outright cruel world. A world crafted to challenge its players, plunging them into an atmosphere of perpetual struggle—for resources, for territory, for survival and freedom. A world where most of its lands remain blank spaces on an unwritten map—a map that, perhaps, awaits your pen.
