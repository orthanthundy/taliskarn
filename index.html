The World of Unwritten Maps


Imagine a cold and gloomy northern highland. Giant moss-covered pines stretch their sullen crowns toward the heavy leaden sky. An eternal haze swirls over the serene surface of white ‚Äì colder than ice ‚Äì waters of a lake lost in these forests. Hundreds of miles to the south lie the endless russet steppes. From horizon to horizon, the eye beholds only a swaying sea of coppergrass, and occasionally, rare table mountains.
